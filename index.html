01 / INTAKE

State of the account

OOFOS came in with strong product-market fit in a category growing at +150% over five years. The marketing engine did not match it. Q1 2025 closed with revenue down 33% YoY and new customer acquisition down 55%. Returning customers were carrying topline. The new-customer funnel was draining.

Our intake analysis surfaced four structural failures:

ChannelFailureScore
Google Ads~70% of spend on brand terms — harvesting demand, not creating it5/10
Meta$800K+ on existing customers, frequency caps blown past 465/10
TikTok28% of budget on retargeting on a discovery-native platform5/10
Linear TVUp to 41% of media mix in peak months — negatively correlated with new cohorts—

The accounts were not broken. They were over-optimized for the wrong objective. Almost every dollar was spent harvesting existing intent. Almost no dollars were spent generating it.

02 / EXECUTION

Three phases, one direction

The plan was structural, not tactical. Phase 1 fixed the engine. Phase 2 captured demand. Phase 3 installed an incrementality-led measurement spine to make the gains durable.

PHASE 01
Rebuild the engine
Q2 2025 · APR—JUN
  • Meta: consolidated 10+ campaigns to 4. Disabled reservation-based delivery. Existing-customer spend cut from 25%+ to 17.7%. DPA share to 50%+ at $28 CAC.
  • Google: consolidated by funnel and seasonality. Removed budget caps. ROAS targets govern delivery, not daily spend limits.
  • TikTok: 100% of spend redirected to new customers. 55+ cohort allocation cut to 21%. Spark Ads with creator handles became core format.
  • DV360: moved from YouTube-only to display, video, and CTV with segmented audiences across publisher and intent layers.

By June, prospecting spend was up +220% YoY in May, +98% in June. The new-customer YoY gap closed from −54% (Q1) to −10% (Q2). Q2 revenue landed at −9.4% YoY versus −33% in Q1.

PHASE 02
Scale the demand
Q3 2025 · JUL—SEP
  • New customers: +117% YoY in August, +105% in September.
  • Revenue: +72% in August, +38% in September.
  • nCAC turned −10.98% YoY in August. First month efficiency moved with volume.
  • End-of-Season Sale exceeded forecasts to the point operations had to throttle volume — warehouse couldn't ship fast enough.

Two operating principles came out of Q3 and have governed every decision since: Paid is for acquisition. Owned is for retention. And: Scale is driven upstream, not manufactured at the point of conversion.

PHASE 03
Pressure test
Q4 2025 · OCT—DEC
  • The 90/95 prospecting split held throughout. No defensive retreat to retargeting under budget pressure.
  • BFCM split into phases. Build-up window started Nov 9 to seed the funnel before promo launch.
  • Black Friday: all-time high revenue, +12.08% YoY. Day 1 of promo period landed +65% above forecast at ~$27 nCAC, ~7x ROAS.
  • Owned channels handled returning-customer volume during loyalty promos. Retargeting spend held flat (varied by less than $400) across the entire promo period.

04 / MEASUREMENT

Incrementality as system of record

At $3.5M+ across seven channels, last-click ROAS is no longer a decision-making tool. It systematically over-credits channels that intercept existing intent (branded search, retargeting). It systematically under-credits channels that build it (CTV, TikTok, upper-funnel programmatic).

If budget allocation is driven by last-click reporting, the channels that build demand lose budget to the channels that harvest it. Eventually there is no demand left to harvest. That was the failure mode at OOFOS in early 2025.

HAUS geo-holdouts installed as the spine

We installed HAUS geo-holdout testing as the measurement spine, complemented by Triple Whale for multi-touch attribution. The methodology is structurally simple. Expose one set of geographic markets to a channel. Hold another set out. Measure the difference in business outcomes. Output: an incrementality coefficient — what revenue would have been lost if that channel hadn't existed.

Budget allocation becomes a P&L optimization, not a platform-reporting comparison.

Q4 2025 — Q1 2026 testing program

  • CTV (Roku, Axon): $307K Q1 2026 with no last-click attribution. Incrementality is the only measurement available.
  • Meta scale: at what spend level does Meta begin diminishing? Modeled directly, not inferred.
  • Cross-channel halo: does TikTok lift Meta CVR? Does CTV pre-load audiences Meta then converts? Tested, not assumed.
  • AppLovin: first HAUS test below 1.5× iROAS target. Interpreted as over-scaling, not structural failure. Reduced spend, kept channel active.
  • Pinterest: October test posted +1.02 iROAS in low-demand conditions. Retest planned early Q2 2026 against 1.5× target.

The decisions HAUS produces are secondary. The primary outcome is that every dollar's marginal return can now be modeled against every other dollar's marginal return on a consistent basis. That is a different operating model from "which campaign has the best ROAS this week."

06 / NEXT

Retargeting, on our terms

The 95/5 split was the right answer for an account where retargeting had hollowed out the new-customer funnel. It is not the right answer forever. With prospecting now producing demand at scale, a small, surgical retargeting layer becomes a precision tool again — not the engine.

The reintroduction is deliberate. It is not a drift back to the old mix.

  • Capped allocation. Single-digit share of paid budget. The job is to convert warm signal, not to manufacture topline.
  • Defined audiences only. Lapsed customers past a defined inactivity window. High-intent visitors who reached PDP or cart and did not convert. No site-wide retargeting pools.
  • Frequency disciplined. Caps set well below 2025 levels — the original failure mode at OOFOS was a frequency of 46 per user. The new ceiling is set to nudge, not to wear down.
  • Incrementality-gated. Each segment runs through a HAUS holdout before scale. If a cohort would have converted without the impression, the spend doesn't earn its slot.

The strategic point is consistent with the rest of the operating system. Retargeting earns budget the same way every other channel earns budget — by proving incremental contribution, not by claiming last-click conversions that would have happened anyway.

The headline outcome was 78% more new customers at 30% lower nCAC. The underlying outcome was an operating system.

The campaigns are interchangeable. The system is not.
